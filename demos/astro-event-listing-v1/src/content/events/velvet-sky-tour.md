Velvet Sky's debut record sat on heavy rotation in the kinds of places that still print zines. Their live show is the reason — half the songs on the record open up another three or four minutes on stage, and the band leans into it.

The Mercury's a 240-cap room with a real soundperson and a stage two feet off the floor. The room is the point. Expect the band to play forty-five minutes, then noodle into something longer for another twenty.

**Cold Static** opens. Local. Two guitars, a synth, a drum machine that has lived an interesting life. **Margot Hayes** plays a short solo acoustic set between bands.

Tickets are tiered — first 50 at $18, then $24 at the door if any remain. Cash bar. No re-entry. The room sells out about half the time on these kinds of bills.
